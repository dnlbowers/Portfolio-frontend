Are you always just a developer?

No, I am obsessed with all things tech, and I do spend many long hours sat at a screen due to love of my craft. However, there is more to the man behind the monitor.

This page is some insight into what I do outside of the coding dojo with each skill feeding back into my passion of learning to create with a keyboard.

How I spend my Free time

Reading

When I'm not coding, you can find often me reading fantasy novels to expand my imagination and non-fiction to expand my logical thought patterns, whilst challenging my own points of view.

Meditating

I have been meditating for a long time. I am far form a master on a mountaintop, but I believe this skill is integral to a happy and creative mind set, with the flexibility to see other point of view outside of my own internal world.

Being a Gigantic Man child

Here I will quote my own mum, "Men don't grow up, they just get bigger." I take pride in making this a life motto. As the proud father of a young boy I never cease to be amazed at the ability Oliver has to find joy and wonder in the simplest of things. Always curious, constantly playful and I get right in there with him. We both enjoy running around the park together and stopping to ponder the never ending questions to exit from a 5 year old's imagination. If you ever find yourself stuck in life, I highly recommend regressing to your inner child, adults can be so dumb ;-)

Martials arts

Although out of practice (and considerably less fit than I was once upon a time) I still love to take the time to slow down, move and breathe. I have studied everything from JuJitsu to Capoeira even including some abstract russian styles that focus on breathe and strength work rather technique and form. Martial arts have taught me discipline and strength of mind which still to this day enables me to overcome challenges on and off the matt.
